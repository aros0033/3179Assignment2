Health Expenditure in Australia

Global Health Expenditure

In line with the global pandemic in 2020 Australia's health expenditure to GDP % peaked at 10.68% putting it 8.08% behind the USA (18.76%).
Furthermore, in 2011 Australia saw its lowest % in this 10 year period (2011-2021), 8.54%.

Tasmania and SA have been the front runners of health expenditure to GSP %, averaging around 0.14% from 2011-2021.
WA has consistently been the lowest performing state in terms of health expenditure

As expected the majority of health expenditure is funded by government entities (State and territory + Australian Government),
with 73% of all funds originating from government entities in 2021.

From 2011-2021 the predominant areas of spending have consistently been primary health care, public hospital services and referred medical services
with an uptick seen in 2020, in line with the global pandemic.
